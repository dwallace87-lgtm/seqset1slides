CASE FILE: COLD WAR

First step: Analyze the source intel. DATES ARE CRITICAL.

DOCUMENT 1:

President Dwight Eisenhower, Press Conference.
APRIL 7, 1954.

DOCUMENT 2:

Tonkin Gulf Resolution, U.S. Congress.
AUGUST 7, 1964.

INITIAL ANALYSIS:

  • The events are exactly a decade apart. What happened in between?
  • This is the height of the COLD WAR.
  • Think: Containment, Vietnam, fear of Communism.

DOCUMENT 1: EISENHOWER'S PRESS CONFERENCE

"...Finally, you have broader considerations that might follow what you would call the 'falling domino' principle. You have a row of dominoes set up, you knock over the first one, and what will happen to the last one is the certainty that it will go over very quickly. So you could have a beginning of a disintegration that would have the most profound influences."

"...But when we come to the possible sequence of events, the loss of Indochina, of Burma, of Thailand, of the Peninsula, and Indonesia following, now you begin to talk about areas that not only multiply the disadvantages that you would suffer through loss of materials, sources of materials, but now you are talking about millions and millions and millions of people."

— April 7, 1954

ANNOTATING DOC 1: KEY INTEL

"...what you would call the 'falling domino' principle... you knock over the first one... the last one... will go over very quickly."

ANALYSIS: This is the core concept—the Domino Theory. It frames Communism as a contagion that will inevitably spread from country to country if not stopped.

"...the loss of Indochina, of Burma, of Thailand, of the Peninsula, and Indonesia following..."

ANALYSIS: Eisenhower is specifically naming the countries at risk. This isn't a vague fear; it's a geopolitical strategy focused on Southeast Asia.

"...you are talking about millions and millions and millions of people."

ANALYSIS: This raises the stakes. It's not just about territory; it's about ideological control over vast populations and resources.

DOC 1 DECODED: THE THEORY OF FEAR

Document 1 is NOT about an action.

It's about establishing the REASON for a future action.

It creates the ideological justification—the FEAR—that will drive U.S. foreign policy in Southeast Asia for decades.

DOCUMENT 2: TONKIN GULF RESOLUTION

"Whereas naval units of the Communist regime in Vietnam... have deliberately and repeatedly attacked United States naval vessels... and Whereas these attacks are part of a deliberate and systematic campaign of aggression..."

"Therefore be it Resolved... That the Congress approves and supports the determination of the President... to take all necessary measures to repel any armed attack... and to prevent further aggression."

— August 7, 1964

ANNOTATING DOC 2: THE GREEN LIGHT

"...naval units of the Communist regime... have deliberately and repeatedly attacked United States naval vessels..."

ANALYSIS: This is the casus belli, or the justification for war. The document claims the U.S. was attacked first, framing America's response as defensive.

"...a deliberate and systematic campaign of aggression..."

ANALYSIS: This language connects back to the Domino Theory. The attack isn't seen as an isolated incident, but as part of a larger, sinister plan of Communist expansion.

"...Congress approves and supports the President... to take all necessary measures..."

ANALYSIS: This is the most critical phrase. It's a "blank check" for war, giving the President broad power to escalate military action without a formal declaration of war from Congress.

DOC 2 DECODED: AUTHORIZATION FOR WAR

Document 2 is the MECHANISM that turns theory into reality.

It provides the LEGAL and POLITICAL cover for military intervention.

It is the direct consequence of the fear established in Document 1, giving the President the power to act.

BUILDING THE CONTEXT

Using the docs and outside intel, we establish the battlefield:

  • The world is locked in the Cold War: USA vs. USSR.
  • The Korean War just ended in a stalemate, proving the communist threat in Asia was real.
  • America's grand strategy is CONTAINMENT - stop communism from spreading, no matter where.
  • The "Domino Theory" from Doc 1 creates a high-stakes environment. The U.S. is primed for a fight.

CONNECTING THE INTEL

How do the two documents relate?

Eisenhower's Domino Theory establishes the motive.

Congress gives the President a green light for war.

THE EFFECT

The relationship is a clear-cut CAUSE AND EFFECT. The fear (Cause) directly led to the authorization for war (Effect).
